Quaid-e-Azam

1876 - 1948

Biography

Quaid-e-Azam, an honorific title meaning "Great Leader," is the revered name given to Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founding father of Pakistan. Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, Jinnah emerged as a prominent leader during the Indian independence movement, advocating for the rights of Muslims in British India. A skilled lawyer and statesman, he played a pivotal role in the creation of Pakistan, the world's first Islamic republic, which gained independence from British rule on August 14, 1947. Serving as Pakistan's first Governor-General until his death on September 11, 1948, Quaid-e-Azam's vision and leadership continue to inspire generations of Pakistanis.
